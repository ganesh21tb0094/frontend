    • Multi-rotor drones: These use multiple propellers (e.g., quadcopters, hexacopters) for vertical takeoff and landing (VTOL). They are highly maneuverable and affordable, making them popular for aerial photography, inspections, and surveillance, though they have limited flight times.

    • Fixed-wing drones: Designed like traditional airplanes, these are more energy-efficient and can cover long distances. They are used for large-scale applications like mapping, surveying, and agriculture but cannot hover.

    • Single-rotor drones: Resembling helicopters, these are more complex and expensive but can carry heavier payloads and offer longer flight times.
      • ,
    • Hybrid VTOL drones: These combine the vertical lift of multi-rotor drones with the efficient forward flight of fixed-wing designs, offering great versatility for applications that require both hovering and long-range flight.
